In celebration of the International Day for the Elimination of Racial Discrimination (21st March), the BEIS FAME Network and the Civil Service Race Forum are pleased to invite you to this virtual event on COVID-19 vaccines hesitancy in BAME communities.

Join us to hear from Nadhim Zahawi MP, Minister for COVID-19 Vaccine Deployment, who will reflect on the deployment of COVID-19 vaccines development, and other diverse speakers who will share a range of perspectives: from an academic view on traditional vaccine hesitancy in BAME communities, to professional reflections on policy development and being on the frontline of the healthcare response; from colleagues sharing their experiences of receiving the vaccine to some reflections from a faith perspective.

Sarah Munby, BEIS Permanent Secretary, will also deliver opening remarks.

Speakers include:

- Nadhim Zahawi MP, Minister for COVID-19 Vaccine Deployment, BEIS
- Sarah Munby, Permanent Secretary, BEIS
- Dr Neha Gadiyar, Junior Doctor, Croydon University Hospital
- Dr Gwenetta Curry, Lecturer on Race, Ethnicity and Health, University of Edinburgh
- Alyson Williams MBE, retired midwife
- Ricky Belgrave, Senior Policy Advisor, Department for International Trade
- Stuart Carrol, COVID-19 Vaccines Taskforce, BEIS
- Yunus Dudhwala, Head of Chaplaincy, Barts Health
- Emmanuel Ibiayo, Deputy Director, BEIS (Moderator)

The joining link will be sent out to registered participants two working days before the event.
